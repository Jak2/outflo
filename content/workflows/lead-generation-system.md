## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Define your perfect customer before reaching out to anyone.

Prompt:
"Help me define my Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) for [YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE].

I want to identify:
1. Industry / vertical (top 3)
2. Company size (employees + revenue range)
3. Job title of the decision maker
4. Top 3 pain points they face daily
5. What they've already tried (and why it failed)
6. Trigger events that make them ready to buy

Be specific. Avoid vague descriptions like 'small business owners.'"

## Prospect Research

Research a specific prospect before writing a single word of outreach.

Prompt:
"I'm about to reach out to [NAME], [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY].

Research and summarise:
1. What does the company do and who are their customers?
2. Recent company news, funding, or product launches
3. Likely pain points given their industry and size
4. Any content [NAME] has published (posts, interviews, articles)
5. A personalisation hook I can use in my first message

Use public information only. Format as a brief research report."

## Personalised Cold Outreach

Write a cold email or DM that feels 1-to-1, not mass-blasted.

Prompt:
"Write a personalised cold [email / LinkedIn DM] for this prospect:

Name: [NAME]
Company: [COMPANY]
Role: [JOB TITLE]
Pain point: [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT]
Personalisation hook: [FROM RESEARCH STEP]

Rules:
- Under 100 words for the opening message
- Lead with their world, not mine
- One specific, low-friction CTA (15-min call / reply with yes-no question)
- No buzzwords, no corporate speak
- Do not start with 'I hope this finds you well'"

## Follow-Up Sequence

Build a 3-touch follow-up sequence that adds value instead of just bumping.

Prompt:
"Write a 3-message follow-up sequence for a prospect who hasn't replied to my first outreach.

Context:
- Product/service: [WHAT YOU OFFER]
- Initial email subject: [SUBJECT LINE]
- Prospect role: [JOB TITLE]

Follow-up rules:
- Touch 1 (Day 3): Add one new piece of value (stat, case study, resource)
- Touch 2 (Day 7): Take a different angle or challenge an assumption
- Touch 3 (Day 14): Polite break-up message that leaves the door open

Each message: under 75 words. Different subject lines."
